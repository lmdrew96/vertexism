I."What is God?" No answer has ever satisfied me.

I was raised with the Abrahamic version of God: Jehovah, the almighty, all-seeing authority above. This monotheism never seemed right to me — for many reasons, not least because the relationship felt very one-directional. I'm down here. He's, supposedly, up there. The imperfect below the perfect; mundane below the divine. Was there not divinity constantly flowing all around me, from all directions?

Polytheism divides divinity up amongst many beings — this feels closer, more fair. However, it still focuses on separation of the divine and the ordinary. Gods are characters with agendas; their connections simply plotlines.

Atheism interested me for a long while; it was honest, and I respected that. But it simply ignored the thing I could never explain: why certain moments felt sacred.

Pantheism got closer to what I was feeling; the belief that God is everything. But no — it wasn't right either. Surely a pebble is not as divine as a loved one's laugh.

Panpsychism gets even closer — it says experience is universal; everything has a point of view. That satisfied my inquiring mind until it didn't. It doesn't tell us why certain moments electrify the air and excite the soul. It still worships solitary things and beings and other nouns.

None of the belief systems I've looked into could explain what really matters to me: what happens when experiences connect. The answer was poetic: what if the divine was never in anything, but between everything?

IV.Connection is the divine.

Yes, a vertex is technically the point where two lines meet. That's the whole idea — a vertex without edges is just a dot floating in empty space. What makes a vertex a vertex is its connections. Every other theology worships the dot. Vertexism says the dot is only a portion of what matters — only a mere product of connection. Every dot is inherently neutral and full of potential; our own free will determines our fate.

You won't find the divine in any one place or thing, but in the spaces between — in the relationships themselves. Sanctity lives in the connections between all experiences.

Edges exist in one of four states.

Latent
Potential connection, not yet activated. The stranger on the bus — two dots in proximity, edge not yet firing. Only one side feels the pull. Pursuing it without reciprocity borders on corruption. Activation requires mutual opening: eye contact, a smile, a conversation.
Tap the edge or the dots

Latent is a connection with potential, like noticing the same interesting stranger on the bus day after day who never seems to notice you.

Sacred edges require mutual awareness, responsiveness, and respect — both nodes must honor the connection.

Residual edges are those between us and ones who are no longer here with us as we are now. We continue to pour love into these connections through remembrance, and in return are met with lingering energy — smells, sounds, memories, signs.

Corrupt edges are connections that are manipulated by one or more nodes to serve themselves; a selfish lack of respect for the edge and the other node.

Divine inspiration does not come from the quantity of outer edges an individual has. Popularity does not make any dot more connected to the divine itself. Rather, the intention to create, maintain, and cherish a wide variety of sacred edges is what is divinely influenced.
